AMDP

AMDP is a widely used tool for assessing psychiatric symptoms and syndromes. It consists of a comprehensive set of items designed to evaluate various aspects of mental health, including mood, thought content, behavior, and cognitive functioning. The scale uses a structured interview format, and responses are scored according to a predefined range, often from 0 (no symptoms) to 4 (severe symptoms). The total score is calculated by summing the ratings across all items.

The AMDP is a copyrighted tool, and its use may be subject to specific licensing requirements depending on the context. Therefore, a value set is provided for proper use in clinical or research settings.
